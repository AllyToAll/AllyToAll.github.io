Widths
Different terrain has different widths. Different widths mean different sized divisions work better. Thus, you should select a division width that on average works best for the terrain it can be expected to fight on.
In order to find out good widths, you can use the chart on this post
Width isn't everything, there is not much gain if you ruin the other statistics that are meaningful just to reduce the penalty by an insignificant amount, but that doesn't mean you should design divisions that due to width perform poorly absolutely everywhere with massive penalties


Artillery
Artillery adds a good quantity of soft attack, which means a good quantity of damage. However, it is expensive.
Support artillery is recommended as it's cheap
Line artillery is more expensive and so keep that in mind


Air

Fighters
Fighters are essential to air supremacy, without which you will lose any other planes in air missions.
They provide a 25% buff to ground combat


CAS
CAS provides a buff of up to 35% to ground combat whilst also contributing damage through ground attack, they are involved in battle at a rate of 3 planes per 1 combat width. The closer you are to the limit, the closer you are to the 35% buff


Navy

Carriers
There used to be a 4 (5 if you cheesed it) carrier limit, this doesn't exist any more, use as many carriers as you want, but instead the planes in combat is limited by the number of enemy ships
Planes on a carrier are 4x as effective as normal planes, I recommend using as many naval bombers as possible as they will be very effective at sinking enemy ships


Capitals
Capitals consist of BB BC CA
Capitals screen carriers at a rate of 1:1 meaning for every carrier you need a capital


Screens
Screens consist of DD and CL
Screens screen capitals at a rate of 4:1 meaning you need 4 screens for every capital


Submarines
Submarines primarily sink enemy convoys
Split off all of your submarines into their own fleet, and split them into small task forces, with an admiral who has traits that work best for submarines.
Do not use submarines in shallow water, they will be spotted and sunk within seconds


Spotting
If you don't want to run out of oil, most of your fleet will be set on Strike Force, which means when you spot a fleet, the strike force will go and hunt it down
However, for that to work better, you can dedicate a few ships specifically to spot the enemy fleet
Light cruisers work best for this as they're cheap, and you can stick float planes on them, as well as sonar and radar, and therefore be able to spot the enemy fleet very easily
